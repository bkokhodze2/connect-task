About GCCI

The Georgian Chamber of Commerce and industry (GCCI) was founded in 1960 and has been committed to supporting the development of business in Georgia ever since. Throughout the years our team has conducted numerous projects and activities to help develop country’s business environment and to support and aid Georgian businesses. The main aim of the charter is to support and protect business entities which operate within Georgia.

The structure of chambers of commerce internationally is associated with trade, thus GCCI closely cooperates with international partners for better support and advises businesses in all areas, including international trade and export. To better mitigate ongoing issues with appropriate stakeholders, GCCI conducts open dialogues with its existing and prospective partners.

We have extensive international network and closely cooperate with international chambers, foundations and businesses of all size. In collaboration with our partners, GCCI has organized various international events and forums to further strengthen established partnerships and offer local businesses an international perspective and acquired experience. The aforementioned activities are few of the examples of chamber’s successful work in this direction. This is the primary reason why Georgian companies prefer membership at GCCI, to receive full benefit of GCCI’s international contacts and established relations. GCCI has signed dozens of Memorandums of Understanding with various international partners and conducts projects under the framework of the MOU.
